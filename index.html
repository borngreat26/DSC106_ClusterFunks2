Interactive RR Interval Analysis from the MIT-BIH Arrhythmia Dataset

Summary

This project analyzes RR intervals — the time between consecutive heartbeats — using data from the MIT-BIH Arrhythmia Database. A preprocessing pipeline extracts and filters beat annotations from raw ECG recordings, then calculates RR intervals to identify and visualize patterns in cardiac rhythms. Users can explore interactive visualizations of heartbeat trends across different records and beat types, including highlighting abnormal runs like ventricular ectopy.

Definitions

  • RR Interval: Time (in ms) between two consecutive R-peaks in the ECG signal.
  • Record: A 30-minute ECG segment from one patient in the MIT-BIH dataset (e.g., Record 100).

Beat Types

  • Normal sinus beat: initiated by the heart’s natural pacemaker.
  • Paced beat: generated by an artificial pacemaker.
  • Left bundle branch-block beat: delayed activation in the left ventricle.
  • Premature ventricular contraction (PVC): early abnormal beat from the ventricles.
  • Fusion of paced & normal beat: overlap between pacemaker and natural signal.
  • T-wave marker / no QRS: not an actual beat, often just a T-wave label.
  • R-peak onset: start of a heartbeat's QRS complex.
  • Atrial premature beat: early beat from the atria.
  • Non-conducted P-wave: atrial signal that doesn't trigger a heartbeat.
